Character Creator

For my ongoing project Magenta, I wanted to have a wide variety of face and bodyshapes for the characters of the setting. I also needed these diversely proportioned characters to share animations, and to do so without using Unity's in-built mechanim humanoid animation rigging system. The method I arrived at was to have every male and female character derive from their own respective character model, and to then modify their appearance through blendshapes. Above you can see the base face mesh from which all male characters are derived.

Pictured above are some examples of the range of the facial customization. Certain features, like eye separation and mouth height, are not fully implemented and so not shown.

The male base model's facial animations are also driven through blenshapes, meaning any facial configuration can seamelessly animate any facial expression.

The character's body type can be controlled with blendshapes as well, allowing characters to have a variable level of bodyfat and muscularity in order to create a realistic variety of characters.

Environment Art HDRP

After working a while on Magenta, I realized to get the look I wanted I'd need to move from Unity's Scriptable Renderer pipeline to it's newer High Definition Render pipeline. I decided to revamp my assets as well as updating the render pipeline. While building these newer assets, I wanted to focus on making smaller levels, with a cleaner but still utilitarian style. I also began to move from painting textures per model towards painting trimsheets and tailoring model UVs to them. That way, I could keep the hand painted look I wanted, while being more time effecient.

Environment Art

When making large, detailed levels for Magenta, I've turned to modularity to give me a large enough asset library to create a convincing city. The huge megastructural mess of the setting lends itself well to haphazard pieces of infrastructure, so my first steps were to create bundles of loose wiring, ductwork, scaffolding and piping. I was inspired by oil refineries and Kowloon Walled City, and wanted players to taste rust and smell damp as they walked through the setting. I split the assets I needed to create into three categories, small and high detailed meshes that served as greebling and visual guides for the player, medium sized and less detailed meshes that served as the main body of the levels, and very large, low detailed meshes which served as background objects meant to bring a sense of scale to the level.
